⚖️ Regulatory & Legal Radar

The most important regulatory development of the week — arguably of 2026 so far — was the SEC and CFTC joint crypto classification guidance issued March 17. For the first time, both agencies agreed on a taxonomy: digital commodities, digital collectibles, digital tools, stablecoins, and digital securities. Crucially, the guidance explains how a non-security token can become subject to securities law (and how it can exit). This resolves a decade-long ambiguity that has kept institutional capital on the sidelines.

The accompanying MOU (announced the same week) formalises coordination via Project Crypto, eliminating duplicative agency registrations and turf wars. Chairman Atkins called it a "roadmap for harmonisation." For the industry, this is unambiguously positive structurally — but near-term it means enforcement is also being harmonised, not relaxed.

Active Legislation

FIT 21 (Market Structure): Passed House, Senate committee markup expected Q2 2026. Defines CFTC vs SEC jurisdiction per new taxonomy.

Stablecoin Act: Senate vote expected late Q2 2026. Bank-issued stablecoins vs non-bank debate ongoing. Key for USDC (Circle) and USDT.

SEC/CFTC MOU: Effective March 17 — Project Crypto coordination now operational. Joint examinations begin Q2.

Enforcement & Upcoming Dates

SEC Clarification (March 17): "Non-security crypto asset" definition now codified — immediate impact on exchange registration obligations.

Q2 2026: First joint SEC/CFTC examination reports expected. Any enforcement actions will be coordinated — higher signal quality.
