🔴 IRAN WAR — Week 5 (Active Risk)

Event: US-Israel military operations against Iran entered their 5th week. Iranian President Pezeshkian reported open to ceasefire (March 30, unconfirmed).

Transmission Mechanism: Iran → Hormuz Strait control → 4.5-5M bbl/day supply loss (5% of global supply) → Brent +55% in March (record) → global stagflation → central bank paralysis → long-duration asset compression + gold/commodities bid. The stock market channel is simple: oil up = earnings down (margin compression across industrials/transport/chemicals).

Critical Mid-April Deadline: Oil industry experts warn Hormuz must reopen by mid-April or supply disruptions double to ~9M bbl/day — the largest oil supply shock in history (greater than 1973 embargo). Societe Generale models $150/bbl Brent under that scenario.

What Reversal Looks Like: Official joint statement from US + Iran governments confirming halt to hostilities → oil down 15-20% on the day → risk-on gap up → tech, airlines, EM equities rerate. The Polymarket 74% ceasefire signal suggests this is the high-probability path by year-end, but timing within Q2 is uncertain.

🟡 FED CHAIR TRANSITION RISK — MAY 2026

Event: Fed Chairman Jay Powell's term expires May 2026. A new chair appointment is pending.

The uncertainty window between now and the appointment is a markets wildcard.
