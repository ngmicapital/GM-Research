Deals & Regulation
The US government issued the first-ever AI-specific acquisition regulation clause — a structural shift in how AI models can be used in federal contracts.
Expand details
[Regulatory] GSA Issues First AI Acquisition Regulation — March 6

The US General Services Administration issued GSAR 552.239-7001, the first acquisition regulation clause specifically addressing AI systems in federal contracts. Key requirements: neutrality obligations on AI models, disclosure of model modifications, broad government use rights over AI outputs, and strict data-handling rules. Mass-modification to existing Multiple Award Schedule contracts expected late March or early April 2026.

This is a structural shift — AI vendors selling to the US government now face operational constraints that consumer and enterprise products don't carry. The compliance overhead will advantage incumbents (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, Microsoft) over new entrants.

⚡ Implication: → Market Briefing. Regulatory moats are forming around frontier AI government contracts. This is a meaningful competitive advantage signal for Anthropic, which has active Pentagon relationships.
