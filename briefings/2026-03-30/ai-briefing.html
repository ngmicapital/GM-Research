Authors: ARC Prize team. Finding: A benchmark for agentic intelligence through novel, abstract, turn-based environments that avoids language and external knowledge. Frontier AI models score below 1%. Humans score 100%.

Why it matters in 6 months: ARC-AGI-2 drove the entire reasoning/o-model wave at OpenAI and the extended thinking push at Anthropic. [Research] DeepSeek mHC — Solving Transformer Training Stability

Title: Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections (mHC). Finding: Solves critical stability issues in the Transformer architecture that cause training runs to degrade at scale — particularly relevant for runs above $100M. If verified by independent replications, this reduces the "lottery" nature of large pretraining runs.

Why it matters in 6 months: Training stability is the silent tax on frontier lab compute budgets. Cheaper, more predictable training → more iteration → faster capability improvement. Watch for adoption signals in the next generation of open-weight releases.

[Research] EPFL Video Generation — Solving Long-Form Drift (ICLR 2026)

[Research] MiroThinker — Interaction Scaling as the Third Axis

Authors: 54-author consortium. Finding: Introduces "interaction scaling" as a third performance axis for AI agents (alongside model size and context length). 81.9% on GAIA benchmark; 72B variant handles 600 tool calls per task.

Practical implication in 6 months: open-source research agents approach or match proprietary ones at a fraction of the cost — viable for automated market research pipelines.

[Research] MemOS + Memory Sparse Attention — Persistent Memory & Linear-Complexity Long Context

MemOS (39-author consortium): A memory operating system unifying plaintext, activation-based, and parameter-level memories for AI systems — enables persistent cross-session memory without full fine-tuning.

Memory Sparse Attention (MSA): Enables LLMs to process extremely long contexts with linear (not quadratic) complexity via sparse attention and document-wise RoPE — dramatically cuts cost for long document workloads.

Practical implication: Both papers point toward the same future — AI that remembers across sessions and processes long trading logs or market histories without context cost penalties.
