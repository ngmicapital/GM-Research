01

Health

PEP: The Metabolite That Explains Inflammaging Mechanistic

A March 2026 Nature Aging paper identifies phosphoenolpyruvate (PEP) — a glycolytic metabolite — as an endogenous brake on the cGAS-STING inflammatory pathway. In both mice and humans, PEP shows a biphasic trajectory: it accumulates early in life, then progressively collapses. When that collapse happens, cGAS-STING goes unchecked, driving the chronic low-grade inflammation ("inflammaging") associated with Alzheimer's, metabolic disease, and accelerated aging phenotypes. In aged humans, high PEP levels correlate strongly with lower inflammation and healthier traits.

Why this matters now: PEP is not yet a commercially supplementable target, but the mechanism points directly at glycolytic health — i.e., metabolic efficiency from resistance training and Zone 2 cardio. The "keep moving" prescription just got a mechanistic foundation. Watch for PEP-modulating interventions in the next 2–3 years. ⚡ New this issue — previously no metabolite-level mechanism for inflammaging this clean.

ARPA-H Commits $166M to Longevity Research Observational

The U.S. government's ARPA-H awarded over $166 million to longevity research via two separate awards in February 2026. This is a signal moment: government capital following basic science into longevity is a precondition for the field graduating from biohacker culture into mainstream medicine. For dc's practical horizon, this means the therapeutic pipeline (senolytics, epigenetic reprogramming, PEP-targeted interventions) is now 3–5 years closer to clinical access than it was 18 months ago.

SleepFM: AI Predicts Disease Risk From Sleep Waveforms Observational

A Nature Medicine paper unveiled SleepFM, a foundation model trained on 585,000+ hours of PSG recordings from 65,000 participants. The model generates sleep representations that predict future disease risk with accuracy exceeding prior biomarkers. The practical read: your sleep quality is a more information-dense signal than almost any other biomarker you're currently tracking. Whoop HRV and sleep staging matter — but they're low-res proxies for what clinical-grade waveform data would tell you.

Hype Watch: Creatine for Inflammation Meta

A 2026 Frontiers in Immunology systematic review and meta-analysis of randomised double-blind placebo trials found that creatine supplementation does not significantly reduce CRP or IL-6 in humans. Moderate certainty evidence. The anti-inflammatory creatine narrative was oversold on mechanistic and endurance-specific data. Keep taking creatine — the strength and cognitive benefits are solid — but don't expect it to fix inflammaging. ⚡ Claim partially reversed this issue.

Aerobic Exercise and Sleep Quality: Dose Threshold Confirmed Meta

A March 2026 systematic review in ScienceDirect confirmed the minimum effective dose for exercise to improve sleep quality: ≥10 weeks, ≥3 sessions/week, ≥30 minutes per session, totalling more than 90 minutes weekly. Low-to-moderate intensity aerobic exercise is sufficient. This is not novel — but having a confirmed threshold is useful for anyone designing a training block. If you're under this dose, sleep benefits are unreliable.

⚡ Highest-Leverage Health Action: Prioritise zone 2 cardio at the minimum effective dose (3×30 min/week) — it simultaneously maintains glycolytic metabolite health (PEP mechanism), hits the sleep improvement threshold, and provides the mitochondrial base for VO2max development.
02

Biohacking

Huberman Lab · Dr. Rhonda Patrick: Vitality & Health Protocols Expert Opinion

The March 23 Huberman Lab episode with Dr. Rhonda Patrick covered sauna, sulforaphane, omega-3s, and deliberate heat/cold exposure as a vitality stack. Patrick's core position: the synergy between heat stress (sauna), cold stress (cold water immersion), and sulforaphane (from broccoli sprouts) activates overlapping longevity pathways — HSP70 upregulation, Nrf2 activation, and BDNF release respectively. These aren't independent tools; stacking them in a weekly protocol compounds the adaptive stress response.

Huberman Lab · Sauna Protocol Essentials (March 12) RCT

Cited research in BMC Medicine confirms cardiovascular mortality benefits from regular sauna use. The episode frames timing as critical: sauna post-workout captures the growth hormone spike amplification effect; sauna within 2 hours of sleep onset is counterproductive (raises core temperature when the body needs to cool for sleep onset). The referenced Acta Physiologica Scandinavica paper on repeated sauna exposure shows endocrine adaptation takes 2–3 weeks of consistent use before the hormonal profile stabilises. Protocol baseline: 3× weekly, 15–20 min at 80–100°C.

Meditation: 5 Minutes Daily Has Measurable Brain Effects RCT

The recent Huberman Lab episode with Dr. Richard Davidson cited evidence that as little as 5 minutes of daily structured meditation improves focus, stress resilience, and measurable health markers. Davidson's lab at Wisconsin produces the most rigorous neuroimaging data on meditation effects. The practical implication: the dose-response curve on meditation is steep and the minimum effective dose is lower than almost anyone implements. Tim Ferriss's current recommendation — The Way app by Zen Master Henry Shukman — is worth trialling for habit formation.

Zone 2 / VO2max Architecture: What 2026 Research Settles Meta

A 2026 scoping review on aerobic exercise and VO2max confirms that Zone 2 alone does not maximally improve VO2max, but provides the mitochondrial density base that makes high-intensity intervals productive. Polarized training (80% Zone 2, 20% VO2max intervals) and non-polarized approaches produce comparable VO2max gains in trained athletes — but polarized has superior recovery economics. The key implication for a PPL program: embed 2–3 Zone 2 sessions in the non-lifting days, and run 1 VO2max interval session per week to drive upward adaptation.

⚡ Stack Adjustment This Week: Add a structured 20-minute post-session sauna protocol (3× this week, post-workout only, not evening), and trial 5 minutes of The Way meditation before market open — both have confirmed minimum effective doses that fit inside the existing daily schedule.
03

Fitness

ACSM 2026 Position Stand: Landmark Guidelines, First Update Since 2009 Meta

The American College of Sports Medicine dropped its most comprehensive resistance training Position Stand in March 2026 — synthesising 137 systematic reviews and 30,000+ participants. The headline finding for hypertrophy: 10 sets per muscle group per week is the evidence-supported volume target. Load intensity (% 1RM) is secondary to total volume for muscle growth — high or low load both work when effort is equated. The most significant sentence in the document: "The best resistance training program is the one you'll actually stick with." This kills most over-engineered periodisation debates. ⚡ First ACSM update in 17 years — significant milestone for evidence-based programming.

Jeff Nippard: Modified Buttermore Split for Maximum Longevity Expert Opinion

Nippard's March 18 content (via glazeopticalsca.com news coverage) covers his modification of the Buttermore Foundation program with a longevity lens: extending recovery windows between identical movement patterns to enable true progressive overload without accumulated joint stress. His data analysis suggests this is especially relevant as training age increases — the recovery window becomes the bottleneck, not the stimulus. For dc's PPL structure, this validates prioritising 48–72 hour separation between the same movement pattern over maximising weekly frequency.

Beginner vs. Experienced Lifter: 365-Day McMaster University Study RCT

Jeff Nippard ran his novice brother and himself on identical programs for 365 days, with measurements from McMaster University using DEXA, ultrasound, BIA, and BioDex. Both tracked every calorie with MacroFactor and sleep with Oura. This is a high-quality n=2 case study — not generalizable alone, but the finding aligns with established literature: beginners gain muscle across almost any program; experienced lifters require more specific volume and intensity manipulation. The message for dc: your gains are now entirely a function of execution quality, not program novelty.

Creatine + Resistance Training: Strength Meta-Analysis Holds Meta

Despite the inflammation narrative collapsing (see Section 01), creatine's strength and muscle mass data remains solid. A 2026 meta-analysis in PMC confirms significant improvements in 1RM and muscle cross-sectional area when creatine is combined with resistance training. The cognitive benefit data in older adults (10g daily, regardless of exercise) also continues to build. Standard dose (5g/day maintenance) remains warranted for any PPL lifter.

What to Change

  • Audit total weekly sets per muscle group — target 10 sets minimum (ACSM 2026)
  • Ensure 48–72 hr between identical movement patterns
  • Add 1 VO2max interval session per week on a non-lifting day
  • Confirm creatine is 5g/day — cognitive and strength rationale both solid

What NOT to Change

  • PPL structure — evidence-supported, no reason to shift
  • Progressive overload as the primary variable — ACSM confirms this trumps complexity
  • Zone 2 cardio on rest days — mitochondrial base building is working
  • Load ranges — high or low load both work when volume is equated
⚡ Gym Implication This Week: Count your working sets per muscle group across last week's PPL — if any muscle group is below 8 sets, add one work set per session before adding new exercises.
